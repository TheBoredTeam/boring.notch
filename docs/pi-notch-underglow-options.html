Notch underglow — two options

Today the running-agent glow is hard-clipped to the notch silhouette (clipShape(NotchShape(bottomCornerRadius: 20))) — so the color ends in a crisp edge at the rounded chin. That edge is the abrupt / mechanical look. Both options below soften it. The notch silhouette itself (black, rounded) is identical in both — only the gradient changes. Toggle the controls; the left panel is locked to A, the right to B, so you can compare straight across.

A · Spill below — the trade-offs

Most like “a gradient layer under the notch, seamless.” The glow lives on the desktop, not inside the cutout.

  • Reads as the notch casting light — softest, least mechanical.
  • Needs a new unclipped layer behind/below the notch in ContentView (the current clipShape trims anything past the chin).
  • Must not capture mouse (allowsHitTesting(false)) and must sit in the window's free space below the closed peek.

B · Inner fade — the trade-offs

Keeps everything where it is today; just changes the gradient so it dies out before the chin.

  • Smallest diff — one gradient + a vertical fade mask in PiPeekView.
  • Glow stays contained in the notch — cleaner, but less of the “spilling underneath” feel.
  • No window-space or hit-testing concerns.
